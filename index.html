The Gospel

The Plan of Salvation

God loves you and has a wonderful plan for your life. No matter where you've been or what you've done, you can know Him personally and experience His grace, forgiveness, and eternal life.

1

God Loves You

God created you, knows you by name, and loves you with an everlasting love. His greatest desire is to have a personal relationship with you — not a distant, religious one, but a real, living friendship.

"For God so loved the world that he gave his one and only Son, that whoever believes in him shall not perish but have eternal life." — John 3:16
2

We Have All Sinned

Sin is choosing our own way instead of God's way. Every person — no matter how good — has sinned and fallen short of God's perfect standard. This sin creates a separation between us and God that we cannot fix on our own.

"For all have sinned and fall short of the glory of God." — Romans 3:23
3

Sin Has Consequences

The consequence of sin is spiritual death — eternal separation from God. But this is not God's desire for you. He sent His Son Jesus Christ to pay the penalty for your sins so that you wouldn't have to.

"For the wages of sin is death, but the gift of God is eternal life in Christ Jesus our Lord." — Romans 6:23
4

Jesus Is the Answer

Jesus Christ, the Son of God, came to earth, lived a sinless life, and willingly died on the cross to take the punishment for your sins. Three days later, He rose from the dead — proving His victory over sin and death. He is the only way to the Father.

"But God demonstrates his own love for us in this: While we were still sinners, Christ died for us." — Romans 5:8
5

You Must Receive Him

Salvation is a gift — it cannot be earned by good works or religious effort. You receive it by turning from your sin (repentance) and placing your faith in Jesus Christ as your Lord and Savior. This is a personal decision only you can make.

"If you declare with your mouth, 'Jesus is Lord,' and believe in your heart that God raised him from the dead, you will be saved." — Romans 10:9
6

You Can Be Sure

Once you trust Christ, God promises you eternal life. This assurance is not based on your feelings or performance — it is based on God's unchanging Word. You can know today that you belong to Him.

"I write these things to you who believe in the name of the Son of God so that you may know that you have eternal life." — 1 John 5:13
✝

A Prayer to Receive Christ

"Lord Jesus, I know that I am a sinner and I need Your forgiveness. I believe You died on the cross for my sins and rose from the dead. I turn from my sins and invite You to come into my heart and life. I want to trust and follow You as my Lord and Savior. Amen."

If you prayed this prayer and meant it from your heart, you have taken the most important step of your life. We would love to hear from you — please submit a prayer request below and let us know!

Google Sheets Setup

Follow these steps once to connect your prayer request form to a private Google Sheet. Takes about 5 minutes.

1

Create a Google Sheet

Go to sheets.google.com and create a new spreadsheet. Name it something like "Hope Through Prayer — Requests". In Row 1, add these headers exactly:

Timestamp · Name · Email · Phone · Best Time to Call · Prayer Request

2

Open the Apps Script Editor

In your Google Sheet, click Extensions → Apps Script. Delete any code in the editor and paste in the following:

function doPost(e) { var sheet = SpreadsheetApp .getActiveSpreadsheet() .getActiveSheet(); var data = JSON.parse(e.postData.contents); sheet.appendRow([ new Date().toLocaleString(), data.name, data.email, data.phone, data.callTime, data.request ]); return ContentService .createTextOutput(JSON.stringify({ result: "success" })) .setMimeType(ContentService.MimeType.JSON); }
3

Deploy as a Web App

Click Deploy → New deployment. Under Select type choose Web app. Set Execute as to Me, and Who has access to Anyone. Click Deploy and authorize when prompted. Copy the Web app URL that appears.

4

Paste Your Web App URL Below

Paste the URL from Step 3 here to connect the form. This is saved only in your browser.

5

Protect Your Sheet (Recommended)

In Google Sheets, click Data → Protect sheets and ranges. Select your sheet and restrict editing to only yourself and any co-admins. This ensures only authorized people can view or edit submissions.
